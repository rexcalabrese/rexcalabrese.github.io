Satellite Dynamics Testbed Development Project

During the development of the testbed, there were two fundamental stages that would have had the most dramatic change on the final product, the method of eliminating friction and the DOF of the device. During the initial stage to determine the best method to eliminate friction, various methods were researched, including, lubricants, magnetic repulsion, mechanical bearings, and air bearings. This first design choice had the greatest impact on the testbed as it would define the constraints for the entirety of the project, for instance, the use of magnetic repulsion would have most likely limited the testbed geometry to a planar system. The second most fundamental choice in the project was the geometry. For air bearings, linear, planar, single/multi-dimensional rotational systems were feasible. The geometric constraints of lower dimensional options such as the spindle offered a simple solution to capture rotation in one axis, though, information about rotation in any other axis was lost. Knowing mass and counter balancing systems would be required, the team chose the spherical air bearing specifically to observe the multi-dimensional data.
